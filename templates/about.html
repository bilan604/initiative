About Me

My experience as a programmer began in University when I learned Python for an internship at IQVIA in my second year. Using Python, along with R and MATLAB, throughout most of my Statistics classes, I also coded a lot as an extracurricular activity. This allowed my experience to span dynamic paradigms such as machine learning, application development, and competitive programming. I genuinely enjoy solving technical problems - and have been interested in Computer Science. I took quite a few challenging CS courses at Davis out of interest, and taught myself Java and C++ outside of school to broaden my understanding of technical concepts. While the code I wrote on Jupyter notebooks for Statistics classes had little to do at a high level with the code I wrote for computer science classes, I noticed that my Jupyter notebooks became 10 times shorter and projects became much easier. Now having full-stack development and Data Analytics / Machine Learning experience, I believe I can succeed in either field, because ultimately what the breadth of my extracurricular experiences taught me was how to find and apply the right tools for the job.
